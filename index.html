Hi, I’m Poonsak Wannawichaikul
by DEK PHUKET

The Calisthenics Athlete ; Learn and Play

I used to be obsessed with basketball. It was my thing -- until a knee injury took me out of the game. It became a chronic issue, something I had to live with. So I started searching for my second sport. That’s when I got into climbing and parkour -- the kind of movements that make you feel alive. But ironically, they were the exact same kind of stress that destroyed my knees in the first place. Then, I discovered Calisthenics. And honestly, it changed my life. It’s been nine years since that day, and I’m still hooked. I love how it builds strength, shapes your body, and most of all -- how damn cool it looks. But every sport has two sides. Even though my knee got better, new pains came -- muscles, joints, shoulders -- because calisthenics is all about upper body control. Still, after years of practice, I’ve learned my body -- I know why I get hurt, and I know how to heal. Nine years in, and I’m still learning, still pushing, still playing. I’ll keep doing calisthenics as long as my body lets me move. Let’s grow, move, and learn together -- one rep at a time.

Progression Review!

Train. Eat. Rest. Then take a moment to reflect -- are you progressing? If yes, how? If not, what needs to change?.

Never forget -- goals are not a straight line. They’re alive. They move with you. They depend on the person who owns this body -- a human being with feelings, emotions, and limits.

The most important thing is to know yourself -- to recognize when to push forward and when to pause. Because understanding that fine line is what keeps you growing, without breaking.

Push!

Get in touch

Ever since the Decathlon Free Gym shut down for renovation -- they say it’ll take about a year -- my crew and I have been training Calisthenics at the Phuket Police Gym instead. It’s not fancy, but it’s got the raw, old-school vibe that pushes you to grind harder every session. The bars are solid, the energy’s real, and the people here are all about progress. If you’re around Phuket and into Calisthenics (or just curious to try), come join us! We usually hit the gym around 6:30 PM and wrap up by 9:00 PM -- no fees, no egos, just pure training and good vibes..
